History

Kenville has a population that is ethnically diverse: roughly equal portions of Indian and Darkie residents, with smaller Coloured ('Brainos') and White communities. English is the most common first language in the area, followed by isiZulu and isiXhosa and a bit of seSotho - reflecting the suburb's multicultural character. This diversity shapes local shops, places of worship, schools and community life. Kenville contains a mix of formal residential properties (standalone houses and apartment complexes) and areas of informal housing (settlements or ‚Äúthe ghetto/the projects‚Äù communities). The suburb and its immediate surroundings include mosques and Hindu temples serving local communities, small commercial strips, and older residential roads that reflect Durban‚Äôs layered urban development. The heart of Kenville is right by the CBD (Kenville's CBD) which is found on Bongani's Street or Bongani's Avenue. The CBD connects most of the Kenville sections/areas, which are Jamaica Section, Ethempeli Section and the main road continues till it reaches the Siyathuthuka, Eringini, and Johanna Road sections.

Recent Events

In recent years Kenville has received media attention because of fires and the vulnerability of informal settlements in the area. Several devastating shack fires have displaced hundreds of residents; these incidents prompted emergency relief and rebuilding efforts involving municipal and provincial agencies. Local infrastructure projects (for example road works) and community safety concerns have also been part of ongoing local political discussions. NGOs and municipal programmes have at times targeted Kenville and neighboring Sea Cow Lake for upgrading and slum-improvement work.

Activities

Sundays are always a blast in Kenville, we have our very own Sunday League... Football tournaments are always on every Sunday at the Veejay Terance Sports Ground. It's an open field suitable for various sports and community events, public access, and used for local sports matches and community gatherings

Health & Safety

For health services, there is our local clinic, which serves the Kenville area located in Smithfield Road. The clinic provides various health services, including occupational therapy, and has been involved in community initiatives such as mural painting to enhance the facility's environment. For safety services, Kenville is served by the Greenwood Park Police Station, it is the closest and most appropriate contact for residents in Kenville

Places of Worship

Diversity is Kenville's middle-name, there's is plenty of places of worship. From the Islamic & Sufi Habibiyah Jamakthana & Madressa (Soofie Saheb Centre) which serves the Muslim community, to the Shembe Nazareth Baptist Church which serves the Shembe community, to the Omega Lighthouse Bible Church which serves the Christian community, to the Shri Vishu Temple Society which serves the Hindu community in the area.

Education

Kenville Primary School, a public primary school serving the local community, offering education from Grade R to Grade 7. And Sea Cow Lake Secondary School, a public secondary school serving the local community, offering education from Grade 8 to Grade 12. The school is committed to providing quality education and fostering personal growth among its learners.
